Biryani

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

INGREDIANTS

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas, potato)
  • 2 large onions, sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • Whole spices (bay leaf, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom)
  • 2 teaspoons biryani masala
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Fresh coriander and mint leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee

steps :

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Cook until 70% done and set aside.
  2. Heat oil/ghee in a pan. Add whole spices and saut√©.
  3. Add sliced onions and fry until golden brown.
  4. Mix in ginger-garlic paste, then add tomatoes and cook until soft.
  5. Add chopped vegetables, turmeric, biryani masala, salt, and yogurt. Cook until vegetables are tender.
  6. Layer half of the cooked rice over the vegetable mixture.
  7. Sprinkle coriander and mint leaves, then add the remaining rice on top.
  8. Cover tightly and cook on low flame (‚Äúdum‚Äù) for 15‚Äì20 minutes.
  9. Turn off the heat, let it rest for 5 minutes, then gently mix.

Paneer Butter Masala

Cooking Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1/2 cup fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves

Steps :

Dal Tadka

Cooking Time: 25 minutes

Ingredients

  • 1 cup yellow lentils (toor dal or moong dal)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 1 onion (chopped)
  • 2 garlic cloves (chopped)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves

Steps :

  1. Wash the lentils thoroughly and cook them in a pressure cooker with turmeric and water for about 3 whistles.
  2. Once cooked, mash the dal slightly with a spoon and keep it aside.
  3. Heat ghee or oil in a pan.
  4. Add cumin seeds and mustard seeds and allow them to splutter.
  5. Add chopped garlic and saut√© until golden.
  6. Add chopped onions and cook until soft.
  7. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  8. Add red chilli powder and salt, then mix well.
  9. Pour the cooked dal into the pan and stir properly.
  10. Let the dal simmer for 5 minutes so all flavors mix well.
  11. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.
